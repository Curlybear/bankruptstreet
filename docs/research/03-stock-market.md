# The Stock Market (Standard rules only)

## Pricing

- **Stock price = 4% of the average shop VALUE in the district** — both the
  starting price and the running floor. The price ticks up whenever 4% of the
  average reaches the next integer. **[V]** (Blue Pichu parts 1 & 3.)
- Investing in shops and shop-value venture cards move the stock price
  *indirectly* through this 4% relationship. Venture cards that name stock
  prices directly "just do what they say". **[V]**

## Buying & selling

- Buy at the **bank** (landing or passing) or a **stockbroker** square
  (landing). **[V]**
- **Up to 99 stocks per district per purchase.** (Mario Wiki phrases it as
  "at one time"; whether total holdings are also capped is contradicted by
  its own +5G × 200 stocks example — treat 99 as a per-transaction cap,
  holdings unbounded. **[U]**)
- **Sell any time before rolling** on your own turn. **[V]**

## Trade price impact — exact rules **[V]** (Blue Pichu part 3)

1. Transactions of **fewer than 10 stocks have no effect** on the price.
2. A transaction of **10 or more** moves the price by
   **floor(price ÷ 16) + 1** — up for buys, down for sells.
3. **Selling more than 10 has the same effect as selling exactly 10** — the
   impact does not scale with size; one step per transaction.
4. Sales can never push the price **below the 4%-of-shop-value floor**.

## Shareholder income

- Stockholders profit from **price appreciation** (and targeted venture
  cards: 10%/20% dividends, price +10/20/30% in a district, stock gifts).
- **No rent-triggered dividend exists**: when rent is paid in a district,
  shareholders of that district receive nothing automatically. **[V]** (No
  source describes such a mechanic; income cards are explicit one-offs.)
- **Boon (20%) and Boom (50%) squares** pay the lander a commission on ALL
  payments made by anyone until their next turn — this is the game's
  "cut of the action" mechanic, tied to squares, not to share ownership. **[V]**
